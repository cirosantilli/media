Download Classical Music

  • American Mavericks - Streaming audio of works by 20th century American composers, including Cage, Cowell, Adams, Babbit, and Copland.
  • Classical Archives - Includes thousands of files (MIDI, MP3, and Windows Media Audio) from over 1000 composers.
  • Classical Piano Midi Page - Includes over 40 MP3 files of classical piano music realized by Bernd Krueger.
  • Classical Piano Short Pieces - MP3 and MIDI recordings of sequenced performances.
  • ELIXIR's MIDI Page for Classical Guitar - MP3 sequences; MIDIs fine-tuned to Roland Sound Canvas SC-88. [Japanese/English].
  • Ian Partridge, Tenor - A few audio and video tracks by Tchaikovsky, Schubert, and Ivor Gurney.
  • J.S. Bach Organ Music - Numerous works performed by various organists on the grand Marbella Organ in Spain.
  • Karadar Bertoldi Ensemble - Popular classical selections in MP3 and MIDI formats.
  • Kunst der Fuge - Thousands of free classical music files (MIDI, MP3, WMA, Real) of fugues and other contrapuntal music.
  • Limburg Cathedral Boyschoir - Choral music by Mozart, Mendelssohn-Bartholdy, Rutter, and Rheinberger.
  • Lisztonian - Classical piano music performed by Jeremiah K. Jones.
  • Mackay-Smith Index - Catalogue of instrumental music includes classical MIDI and WAV files.
  • Michael's Classical Piano Site - MP3 and MIDI files of classical piano music.
  • Opus100 - Streaming RealAudio excerpts of many classical works.
  • Russian Art Songs - MP3 and RealAudio selections from several centuries of Russian vocal chamber music.
  • SuperConductor - Classical music performances created with SuperConductor software.
  • Tubalate - Tuba and euphonium quartet shares RealAudio excerpts from their CDs.
  • Wikipedia - List of free classical music recordings, mostly in ogg format, available at Wikipedia.







MUSIC GENRES

  • All Music Guide - Portal built around articles by music journalists describing music genres, with links and reviews.
  • Audiogalaxy: Glossary of Musical Styles - Hundreds of sub-genres defined in non-technical language mentioning sample artists. Organized under headings such as pop, hip-hop, jazz, blues, classical, Latin, modern rock, rock, and heavy metal.
  • Christian Music Place: Artists by Genre - About two dozen musical genres including blues, a capella, dance/techno, and Southern gospel, with as many as several hundred links to Christian music artists in each.
  • DataDragon: Music Genre Sampler - Children's site with simple definitions of a few genres including rock, Celtic, and classical with artist and site links.
  • Ectophiles Guide by Genre - Self-described "Guide to Good Music" from a group founded in 1991 to support the music of singer Happy Rhodes. Links to artists, almost entirely female vocalists, sorted by genre such as pop, blues, experimental, performance art, beautiful and fierce, and traditional.
  • Indiana University School of Music: Genres - Categorized links organized by researchers at the William and Gayle Cook Music Library. Categories include 20th-Century music, ancient music, band music, tango, flamenco, ragtime, and choral music.
  • Music Classification by Genre: System Performance - Research project completed in 2003 by Mitali Banerjee at Rice University used automatic process to determine musical genre of audio samples.
  • Music Web Hunter: Styles & Genres - Ken Davies has grouped resources in five categories: classical, folk/ethnic/world, jazz, musicals/operas/theater, and pop/rock/country.
  • Wikipedia: Musical Genre - Brief explanation of the way styles can be defined by region, chronology, technical requirements, marketing trends, or the ideas of critics. Extensively linked to sub-genres and examples of significant artists.

COMPETITIONS

  • World Federation of International Music Competitions   - Organization founded in Geneva, Switzerland in 1951 offers worldwide database of classical performance competitions searchable by city, sponsoring organization, and name of winner. With downloadable yearbook [PDF].
  • Bartok-Kabalevsky-Prokofiev International Piano Competition and Festival - Page from Radford University in Virginia documents current and past competitions, sponsored by the IBLA Foundation in New York.
  • BBC Cardiff Singer of the World - Competition for young opera singers established in 1983 and held every other year in Wales, with scheduled performer, past winners, and ticket information.
  • BBC Young Musician of the Year - Biennial competition, with link to applications and results of previous events.
  • Chopin Society of Houston - Sponsoring the International Chopin Youth Competition and the Adult Amateur Competition, with links to past and future events.
  • IBLA Grand Prize - Annual competition for pianists, vocalists, instrumentalists and composers with no age limit, held at Ragusa-Ibla, Sicily. [English and Italian]
  • International Concert Alliance - Hosting mid-year music competitions in New York City in several categories, for cash prizes and Manhattan concert appearances. Dates, applications, and news, with judge profiles and donation information.
  • International Music Competitions of the Val Tidone - Annual event judging piano, accordion, chamber music and composition at the Val Tidone castles outside Milan, Italy.
  • International Paulo Cello Competition - Helsinki, Finland event offers rules, schedule, participants, tickets, application form, cash prizes, history, archives, and contact information. [English and Finnish]
  • International Singing Contest Francisco Viñas - Opera competition held in Barcelona, Spain. Site includes rules, official prize listing, and an online registration form.
  • International Young Musician Competition - Event in Barletta, Italy offers divisions for piano, other solo instruments, chamber music and composition. With links to auditions, schedule, rules, entry fees, prizes, and application form.
  • Kathleen Ferrier Singing Awards - Held each April at Wigmore Hall in London, to benefit memorial scholarship fund named for the British lyric contralto.
  • Metropolitan Opera National Council Auditions - Event held annually at fifteen locations in the US and Canada, followed by finals in New York for cash prizes. Eligibility requirements, judges, and applications.
  • Queen Elisabeth International Music Competition of Belgium - Competitions for violinists, pianists, composers and singers held in Brussels.
  • Tansman Competition - International competition held in Lodz, Poland, named for 19th century Polish composer Alexsander Tansman. Categories for clarinet, piano, violin, cello, and guitar. [English and Polish]
  • Tibor Varga International Violin Competition - Held each summer in Switzerland. History of the competition, program information, rules and regulations.
  • William C. Byrd Young Artist Competition - Annual competition in Flint, Michigan, rotating through categories for piano, strings, voice, and winds and brass.
  • Zinetti Competition - International chamber music event held in Sanguinetto, near Verona, Italy.

Classical Music Resources

  • Classical Net    - A collection of information and news: articles, CD reviews, composers and their music, the basic repertoire, recommended compositions and recordings.
  • Cadenza - "Resources for classical and contemporary musicians": Basil Ramsey programme note library, multi-lingual classical music glossary, musicians' directory, international events list (sorted by country).
  • Chamber Music 2000 - Project initiated by the Schubert Ensemble of London to commission a large number of works for piano and strings from leading composers, suitable for playing by young and amateur musicians.
  • Chamber Music America - National association of professional chamber music provides links, calendars, email lists, publications, and grants.
  • The Classical Buffet - Features biographies, articles, and other information on classical music.
  • Classical Music at About.com - Articles, links, news, chat room.
  • Classical Music Cube ... The Music Beat - Links: basic repertoire, record companies, music history.
  • Classical Music Navigator - Brief information on composers, notable works, and forms and styles of music.
  • Classical Music Thematic Catalogers Index - Catalog of thematic catalogers (of composers' complete works), with links where available online.
  • ClassiCat for Windows from TDWare - (Music collection cataloging software) shareware download, ordering information.
  • Ensemble - Covers all areas of music as taught in school, and sung or played in churches, synagogs, and communities: Jazz, the classics, pop and show; choir, band, or orchestra.
  • Essentials of Music - Basic information about classical music, including eras, terms and composers, with audio examples.
  • Karadar Bertholdi Classical Music Dictionary - Dictionary of composers with brief biographies, catalogs of works, sound files: MP3, MIDI, and opera libretti.
  • The Music Chamber - Resource covering chamber music. Includes instruments, music theory, historical periods, composers, repertoire and audio samples.

FESTIVALS

  • Amherst Early Music - Teaching music festival for historical performance in Massachusetts.
  • Aspen Festival - Outdoor event which lasts for nine weeks in the summer.
  • Bach Festival - America's oldest collegiate Bach Festival featuring internationally acclaimed artists. (Berea, Ohio)
  • Bach Festival of Philadelphia - 25-year-old festival of Baroque music.
  • Bard music festival - Organized by the Bard College (Annandale-on-Hudson, NY)
  • Bellingham festival - Summer event in Washington.
  • Boston Early Music Festival - Critically-acclaimed biennial festival of early period music.
  • Boulder Bach Festival - Dedicated to the music of Johann Sebastian Bach. Held annually in Boulder, CO.
  • Brisbane Festival - Official site of the festival in Queensland, Australia.
  • Brookhaven Choral Festival - Choral festival and work shop held every July in Brookhaven, New York.
  • Carmel Bach Festival - 3 week annual summer event with an emphasis on baroque orchestral and choral music. Carmel, California.
  • Central Vermont Chamber Music Festival - Presents two weeks of concerts in Randolph, VT annually in August.
  • Chamber Music in Oklahoma - A non-profit organization bringing chamber music ensembles to Oklahoma City. Lists schedules, performers, venue and ticket information.
  • Charlottesville Chamber Music Festival - Two weeks of chamber music concerts during September at the Jefferson Theater in Charlottesville, Virginia. Contains ticket information and performer profiles.
  • Cincinnati Flute Symposium - A week long flute seminar where many 'flute masters' participate in the class via recitals and masterclasses.
  • Cooperstown Chamber Music Festival - Features internationally acclaimed musicians. Artist biographies, schedule, and ticket information.
  • Crested Butte Music Festival - An annual four-week summer event with a year-round performance schedule. Takes place in Crested Butte, Colorado. Site provides a schedule, history, ticket information, and accommodation information.
  • Festival of the Sound - Enjoy classical, contemporary, and jazz. Annually held in Parry Sound, Ontario.
  • Florida International Festival - A biennial festival in Daytona Beach, FL that features that world-famous London Symphony Orchestra.
  • Garth Newel Music Center - An annual festival which takes place at an estate in the mountains of Virginia which is dedicated to the appreciation of classical music.
  • Gilmore International Keyboard Festival - Biennial festival of world-famous keyboard musicians performing Classical and Jazz music. (Kalamazoo, MI)
  • Grand Teton Music Festival - Over 40 summer concerts situated at the gateway to Yellowstone park, with lectures and children events. Site also includes a wine auction.
  • Green Lake Festival of Music - A summer series of fine music concerts and workshops, held in a resort area situated in Green Lake, Wisconsin.
  • The Hampstead and Highgate Festival - UK based indoor and outdoor festival, with concert and recital schedule and ticket information.
  • The Honest Brook Music Festival - Presenting classical music recital and chamber performances in July and August in a rural barn in Meredith, NY. Also presenting salon-style classical performances in private homes in NYC and Delaware County NY in January, February, and March.
  • Indianapolis Early Music Festival - The oldest festival in the U.S. with early and baroque music.
  • Miami Piano Festival - An annual piano music festival showcasing international talents.
  • Midsummer Mozart Festival - California-based Mozart festival conducted by George Cleve.
  • Midsummer's Music Festival - Several-week-long music festival taking place in Door County, Wisconsin.
  • Moab Music Festival - An annual series of chamber music concerts in Moab, Utah. Schedule, program information, musician profiles, newsletter, and ticket information.
  • 2nd International Choral Festival - Choral Festival includes evening concerts with profane, sacred and folk music with selected choirs from all over the world. Held in Ljubljana, Slovenia.
  • Orchestrades Universelles - A unique festival of music for young musicians from the whole world. Held in Brive, France.
  • Oregon Bach Festival - Conductor Helmuth Rilling leads choral and orchestral works, chamber concerts, educational programs, and social events.
  • Ottawa chamber music festival - Two-week summer program featuring local, national and international artists.
  • Portland Chamber Music Festival - Held annually during August in Portland, Maine.
  • Victoria Summer music festival - Summer season of chamber music concerts in Victoria, Canada.
